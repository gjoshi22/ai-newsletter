The fastest prototypes start with constraints, not prompts.

Before opening a model, write down the user, the task, the available data, the primary action, and what must never happen. That gives the model something sharper than a vibe to work with.

My preferred loop:

- Sketch the screen in words.
- Generate the first interactive version.
- Remove anything that feels like a landing page.
- Tighten the data states.
- Test the workflow in the browser.
- Only then polish the visual layer.

The goal is not to get the model to design for you. The goal is to make iteration cheap enough that better ideas survive.
